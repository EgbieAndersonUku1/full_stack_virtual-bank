Virtual Bank Employee Handbook

1. Purpose of This Document

This document outlines the expected standards, behaviours, and operational procedures for all employees of the Virtual Bank.

All employees must read and comply with this document.

2. Policy Scope

These policies apply to all employees, contractors, temporary workers, and authorised personnel operating within the Virtual Bank platform.

Additional department-specific procedures may apply depending on role, access level, and regulatory responsibilities.

3. Code of Conduct

Employees must:

  • Treat customers and colleagues with respect and fairness.
  • Act honestly and professionally at all times.
  • Disclose conflicts of interest immediately.
  • Follow internal procedures and escalation paths.

Employees must not:

  • Misuse customer data or access unauthorised information.
  • Accept gifts or incentives that influence decisions.
  • Engage in discriminatory or inappropriate behaviour.
  • Share confidential internal information externally.

4. Data Protection & Confidentiality

The Virtual Bank operates under strict data protection standards aligned with UK GDPR principles.
  • Customer data must only be accessed on a need-to-know basis.
  • All data must be stored securely in approved systems.
  • Passwords must never be shared.
  • Lock screens when leaving workstations.
  • Report suspected data breaches immediately.

5. Customer Service Standards

  • Respond to queries within defined service timeframes.
  • Communicate clearly and professionally.
  • Take ownership of customer issues until resolved.
  • Verify customer identity before sharing account details.

6. Financial Crime & Fraud Prevention

Zero tolerance policy applies to all forms of financial crime.
  • Monitor transactions for suspicious activity.
  • Report fraud immediately using internal tools.
  • Follow AML and KYC procedures at all times.
  • Complete enhanced checks for high-risk customers.

7. System Access & Security

  • Access only systems required for your role.
  • Multi-factor authentication is mandatory.
  • No unauthorised software installations.
  • All activity is logged and auditable.

8. Acceptable Use of Company Systems

  • Company systems must only be used for authorised business purposes.
  • Employees must not attempt to bypass security controls.
  • Unauthorised sharing of internal material is prohibited.
  • Use of personal storage devices may be restricted.

9. Work Conduct & Communication

  • Use official communication channels only.
  • Maintain professional tone in all communications.
  • Do not share sensitive data in unsecured systems.
  • Attend all mandatory training sessions.

10. Incident Reporting

  • Security breaches
  • Fraud or suspicious transactions
  • Serious customer complaints
  • System failures affecting operations

All incidents must be reported within 1 hour where possible using the Internal Incident Management System.

11. Disciplinary Action

  • Verbal warning
  • Written warning
  • Suspension of access
  • Formal investigation
  • Termination of employment

Serious breaches may also be reported to regulatory authorities.

12. Mandatory Training Requirements

  • Annual AML and fraud awareness training.
  • Data protection and GDPR compliance training.
  • Security awareness and phishing prevention training.
  • Role-specific operational procedure training.

Employees may temporarily lose access to internal systems if mandatory training requirements are not completed within required timeframes.

13. Acknowledgement

By accessing internal company systems, employees confirm that they have read, understood, and agreed to comply with all policies, procedures, and compliance obligations outlined within this handbook.

Failure to comply with internal policies may result in disciplinary action, suspension of access privileges, or formal investigation.
